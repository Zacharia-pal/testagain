Insurance Data Warehouse — Patronale Life

Client: Patronale Life NV — Brussels, Belgium
Role: Data Analyst, Developer
Period: March 2025 – Present

Overview and Challenge

Patronale Life NV's mortgage broker operations generated data across multiple systems, but there was no centralized repository to consolidate, transform, and report on this information. Business stakeholders lacked a reliable, unified view of operational performance, and generating reports required manual extraction and reconciliation from disparate sources. The organization needed an enterprise-grade data warehouse that could serve as the foundation for analytics, dashboarding, and data-driven decision-making across the mortgage broker division.

Our Approach

We designed and built a PostgreSQL data warehouse from the ground up, hosted on AlmaLinux infrastructure. The architecture followed an ELT (Extract, Load, Transform) pattern, allowing raw data to be ingested efficiently from source systems before being transformed into analytical models within the warehouse. This approach provided flexibility for evolving business requirements and reduced the complexity of the ingestion layer.

Apache Airflow was selected as the orchestration platform for all data pipelines. We developed custom DAGs (Directed Acyclic Graphs) to automate the extraction of data from source systems, loading into the warehouse, and execution of transformation logic on a scheduled basis. Each pipeline was designed with monitoring, alerting, and retry logic to ensure reliability. Data models were carefully designed to support the specific reporting needs of the mortgage broker operations, with attention to cross-system data consistency between Odoo, AFAS, and other internal platforms.

Dashboards were built on top of the warehouse to provide stakeholders with self-service access to key performance metrics. Reporting automation replaced manual processes, delivering scheduled reports to the teams that needed them without intervention.

Technologies Used

  • PostgreSQL (data warehouse)
  • Apache Airflow (pipeline orchestration and DAG development)
  • Python (ETL scripts, data transformation, automation)
  • SQL (data modelling, queries, and transformations)
  • Linux — AlmaLinux (server infrastructure)
  • Git (version control)

Key Deliverables

  • PostgreSQL data warehouse with ELT architecture
  • Apache Airflow DAGs for automated data pipelines
  • Data models optimized for mortgage broker operations reporting
  • Automated dashboards and performance reports
  • Cross-system data consistency framework
  • Pipeline monitoring and alerting configuration

Results and Impact

The data warehouse transformed Patronale Life's ability to understand and optimize its mortgage broker operations. Stakeholders gained access to reliable, up-to-date metrics through automated dashboards, replacing hours of manual report generation. The ELT architecture provided a scalable foundation that could accommodate new data sources and reporting requirements as the business evolved. Data consistency across systems improved significantly, and the automated pipelines ensured that reports were always current and trustworthy.

Warehouse Management — Gorillas Technologies

Client: Gorillas Technologies — Brussels, Belgium
Role: Warehouse Operations Manager
Period: July 2021 – October 2022

Overview and Challenge

Gorillas was a fast-growing, Berlin-based rapid grocery delivery startup that expanded aggressively into major European cities, including Brussels. The Brussels operation needed to deliver groceries to customers within minutes, which demanded highly efficient warehouse operations and a well-coordinated fleet of riders. Managing a team of 30 to 50 riders in a high-pressure, fast-paced environment required strong operational leadership, precise scheduling, and the ability to adapt quickly to fluctuating demand. The challenge was to maintain service quality and delivery speed while scaling the team and optimizing warehouse throughput.

Our Approach

Starting as an Operations Coworker and progressing through Supervisor to Warehouse Operations Manager, we developed a deep understanding of every layer of the operation. This ground-up experience informed a management approach rooted in practical knowledge of the workflows, pain points, and opportunities for improvement at each level.

As Warehouse Operations Manager, we took full responsibility for the daily operations of the Brussels warehouse. This included planning and managing rider schedules to ensure adequate coverage during peak and off-peak hours, overseeing inventory management and warehouse organization, and handling HR responsibilities for the rider team. Operational statistics were created and tracked to monitor performance, identify trends, and inform staffing decisions. The goal was to ensure that every order was picked, packed, and dispatched within the tight timeframes that defined Gorillas' value proposition.

Key Deliverables

  • Full operational management of Brussels warehouse
  • Rider scheduling and workforce planning for 30–50 team members
  • HR management for rider operations
  • Operational statistics and performance tracking
  • Inventory management and warehouse organization
  • Last-mile delivery coordination and quality assurance

Results and Impact

Under this management, the Brussels warehouse maintained consistent delivery performance in a demanding, high-volume environment. The structured approach to scheduling and workforce planning ensured that rider coverage matched demand patterns, minimizing idle time while avoiding understaffing during peak periods. Operational statistics provided the data foundation for continuous improvement, enabling evidence-based decisions on staffing, process adjustments, and resource allocation. The progression from entry-level to management within the same operation demonstrated adaptability and a strong capacity for operational leadership in a startup environment.

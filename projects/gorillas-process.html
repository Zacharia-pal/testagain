Process Optimization — Gorillas Technologies

Client: Gorillas Technologies — Brussels, Belgium
Role: Business Analyst / Project Manager
Period: Approximately 7 months (within July 2021 – October 2022)

Overview and Challenge

As Gorillas scaled its rapid grocery delivery operations in Brussels, the speed of growth outpaced the maturity of internal processes. Manual HR tasks consumed significant time, operational workflows had redundant steps, and there was limited visibility into performance data. The organization needed someone to step back from day-to-day operations and take a systematic look at how things could be done better. The challenge was to identify inefficiencies, design improved processes, and implement changes without disrupting the fast-moving delivery operations that customers depended on.

Our Approach

We led a series of process improvement initiatives focused on three pillars: operations streamlining, HR automation, and data-driven decision-making. Each area was approached methodically, starting with observation and documentation of the current state, followed by analysis to identify waste and bottlenecks, and concluding with the design and implementation of improved workflows.

For HR automation, we identified repetitive administrative tasks — onboarding paperwork, schedule management, time tracking — and simplified or automated them to reduce the administrative burden on managers. Operational processes were mapped end-to-end, and unnecessary steps were eliminated or consolidated. Where manual handoffs created delays or errors, we introduced standardized procedures and clearer accountability.

On the analytics side, we created comprehensive statistics for rider operations, tracking key metrics such as delivery times, rider utilization, order volumes, and peak patterns. This data was analyzed to identify trends and inform operational decisions, transforming the management approach from intuition-based to evidence-based.

Key Deliverables

  • End-to-end process mapping and optimization for delivery operations
  • HR task simplification and automation
  • Operational statistics framework for rider performance
  • Data analysis and trend reporting for management
  • Standardized procedures and workflow documentation
  • Process improvement recommendations and implementation

Results and Impact

The process optimization initiatives delivered tangible improvements across the Brussels operation. HR administrative tasks that previously required significant manual effort were streamlined, freeing up management time for higher-value activities. Operational workflows became leaner and more predictable, reducing errors and improving throughput. The rider operations statistics provided management with clear visibility into performance, enabling proactive adjustments to staffing and scheduling. These data-driven improvements contributed to a more efficient, scalable operation during a period of rapid growth for the company.
